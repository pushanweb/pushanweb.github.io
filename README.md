## Research Experience

### A Multi-Dimensional Arithmetic-Sum based Fault Tolerant Scheme (Feb, 23-Feb, 24)
This study introduces a comprehensive scheme to protect data integrity in complex 3D tensor spaces, addressing the challenges posed by high-dimensional tensor operations. Conventional fault tolerance techniques, such as error-correcting codes, are inefficient for tensor algebra calculations. The study proposes a collection of different algorithm-based fault tolerance (ABFT) algorithms for addressing thirteen fundamental tensor algebra operations for a 3x3x3 cube. The scheme optimizes these calculations using their algorithmic semantics and finds the most optimal combination. The study evaluates the scheme's capability to detect and isolate errors in tensor computations, showcasing its scalability and adaptability across various application domains.

### Bitcoin and Gold Price Prediction using Hybrid ARIMA and RNN model (Sep, 22-Feb, 23)
This study introduces a novel hybrid forecasting model, combining the robust methodologies of ARIMA and LSTM with Linear Regression for time series forecasting. The model aims to enhance the accuracy of time series forecasts, particularly for Bitcoin price prediction. ARIMA captures temporal dynamics and transient variations, while LSTM models model long-term dependencies and past pricing trends. The hybrid model merges ARIMA and LSTM outputs through Linear Regression, enhancing forecasting precision. The model's performance is evaluated against conventional ARIMA and LSTM models using historical time series data. The results show that the hybrid ARIMA-LSTM-Linear Regression model surpasses individual models in prediction accuracy, highlighting the potential of hybrid modeling approaches in improving time series forecasting precision.
